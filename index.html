Three Pallet Types. One Decision.

Match the right heavy duty pallet configuration to your operation before you order:

Nestable

Stacks 10:1 empty. Best for closed-loop ops where return freight cost is the priority. Lower load rating — ideal for lighter goods up to 3,000 lb dynamic.

Rackable

Engineered for selective and drive-in racking. Structural runners span the beam — no sagging under 5,000 lb. The standard for warehouse racking systems.

Stackable

Rigid deck and base for high-density floor stacking. Stacks 6 high loaded. Preferred for export containers and floor-stacked DC storage.

Why Heavy Duty Operations Switch from Wood

Wood pallets have served warehouses for 80 years — but they were designed for an era before HACCP, ISPM 15, racking systems, and sustainability reporting. The hidden costs of wood pallets accumulate quickly in any serious operation.

Splinters & Contamination

Wood fibre contamination is the number one cause of product rejection in food-grade warehouse audits. A single splinter in a food product or pharmaceutical package triggers a recall. Plastic pallets eliminate the source entirely.

Inconsistent Dimensions

Used wood pallets vary by up to 2 inches in height and deck flatness. This causes conveyor jams, automated pick errors, and label scanner misreads. Plastic pallets maintain ±3 mm dimensional tolerance across every unit.

Moisture Absorption

A standard wood pallet absorbs 4–8 lb of water in a wet dock environment. This extra weight raises freight cost, harbours mould and bacteria, and can cause loads to shift unpredictably on forklift tines. PP pallets absorb zero moisture.

Compatibility: Forklifts, Pallet Jacks & Conveyors

Heavy duty plastic pallets are designed to work with every standard handling system. The 48×40 GMA footprint is the North American standard; 1200×1000 mm and 1200×800 mm (Euro) sizes are also available from Elipacko. Here's what to verify for your specific equipment:

Counterbalance Forklifts

All 4-way entry plastic pallets accept standard 6-inch tines. Specify tine-clearance height (minimum 3.5 inches from floor to underside of deck) when ordering nestable designs.

Pallet Jacks (Manual & Electric)

Rackable and stackable pallets include full perimeter base entry for pallet jack wheels. Nestable pallets typically have partial entry — confirm wheel clearance before deploying on pallet jacks.

Roller Conveyors

Deck flatness tolerance of ±3 mm ensures smooth conveyor transit. Specify deck surface (open-deck / solid-deck / anti-slip) based on your conveyor roller pitch and product type.

Selective & Drive-In Racking

Rackable PP pallets include structural runner beams rated to span standard 42-inch beam centres. Confirm beam span and load per beam with Elipacko before specifying for high-bay racking systems.
