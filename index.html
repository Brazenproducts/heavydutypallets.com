Handling Equipment Compatibility

Forklift & Pallet Jack Compatibility Guide

Heavy duty plastic pallets are designed to work with the same material handling equipment as standard wood pallets, but without the unpredictability of boards that warp, nails that protrude, or edge boards that crack and jam equipment. The consistent geometry of injection-molded and structural foam pallets means your operators spend less time dealing with pallet problems and more time moving product.

For automated warehouses integrating AGVs or conveyor pallet transport systems, plastic pallet geometry is particularly critical. AGV navigation systems rely on predictable pallet dimensions for fork alignment. A wood pallet with a warped deck or broken board can cause a miss that halts the entire picking system. Plastic pallets eliminate this failure mode entirely, making them the default choice for warehouses investing in automation infrastructure.

The load consistency of heavy duty plastic pallets also matters during racking operations. A plastic rackable pallet places its runner load consistently on rack beams every cycle. Wood pallets, especially used ones, may have crown or warp that creates uneven beam contact and uneven stress distribution on the rack structure. Over time, this contributes to rack fatigue and potential failure. Plastic pallet geometry keeps your rack operating as designed.

  • Standard counterbalance forklifts - full compatibility with all pallet types, 4-way entry standard
  • Reach trucks - rackable pallets only; verify deck clearance with your rack bay depth specification
  • Order pickers - stackable plastic pallets work well with order picker fork geometry
  • Electric pallet jacks - rackable and stackable models; nestable types may require wheel well clearance check
  • Manual pallet jacks - all types compatible; plastic pallet consistency reduces tire hang-up on joints
  • AGV systems - plastic pallets strongly preferred; confirm AGV vendor approved pallet list before ordering
